The Stars Among Us is a unique virtual reality experience. It draws inspiration from the vastness of space, and a love of constellations. Utilizing Photoshop and A-Frame, a hardworking team constructed a mini 3D world to explore.


Designed by Kai Hughes, Celine Napkil, Jasey Chanders, and John Robert, The Stars Among Us draws upon a fascination of the night sky. We wanted to create the ideal setting for someone to perceive the wondrous cosmos: a small, quiet garden with no light pollution.

From concept sketches to searching for free 3D assets online, the idea started to evolve and take form. Originally, we planned to have statues in the garden, but settled on just a few simple rocks and plants to minimize distraction from the real star of the show, the constellations. A-Frame was used to place 3D assets into the world, then Photoshop to create an equirectangular image for the sky. This meant one can physically look up and around at each unique constellation we drew.

After our world was ready, it was displayed live in the University of Colorado Boulder's Blackbox Theater. This footage was compiled into a succinct video used to document the final product. The full video can be found by clicking this link.
