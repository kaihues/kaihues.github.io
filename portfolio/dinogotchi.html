Dinogotchi is a fun interactive toy! He's fully wired up so you can nurture and feed him. He's programmed using a Micro:bit, his hardware is soldered, and his body was 3D printed.


Atlas Zaina and I were tasked with creating some sort of interactive, creative object. So, we decided to design the dinogotchi, a tamagotchi-esque interactive toy. The dinogotchi is sitting on his back legs, with a big smile. Each of his arms have a button in the center, and his mouth has a screen in the middle. He was 3D printed and is, as a result, bright blue and black, as we were unable to print him entirely in the same color. We jokingly said he looked like he was frankensteined together, but decided to roll with it as a theme. He has stitches on the seams between two different colors, and lots of spots. At just under 9 inches tall, he's a small friend.

His inner mechanisms are made from a microbit board, wires, two large buttons we came across, a prototyping board, and a micro-usb cable. The cable provides power to the microbit board, which has a grid of LEDs on it. These LEDs are programmed to light up in different patterns to emulate a screen.

Our dinogotchi is intended to be a source of entertainment. Both my groupmate and I have fond memories of tamagotchis, so we wanted to draw inspiration from our nostalgia while also creating something new. Upon turning on the toy, he will display a heart symbol, since he is happy to play with you. One of his buttons allows you to feed him, after which he will go to sleep. The other button allows you to hydrate him, which will also cause him to fall asleep afterwards. If you tilt him to the left, he will display a music note. If you tilt him to the right, he will display a different music note. This is how you play with him and keep him happy.

For more information on the design process and to see a demo video, view my work in progress blog post.
