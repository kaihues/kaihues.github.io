solar-stones.stl

Solar Stones is part of a collaboration centered around the study of the sun. Our team re-created rock carvings used to study ancient astronomy. The carved tablets were 3D printed, molded, cast in resin, then implemented into a larger educational exhibit.




Chaco Canyon is located in northwestern New Mexico. It is the home of the current Puebloan people, descended from the Ancient Puebloan people who inhabitated the area 1000 years ago. It's been documented that these people studied and tracked the sun, using it to guide festivities and keep track of time. The current Puebloans have allowed us to document and re-create replicas of some of their solar petroglyphs, which are an important part of their culture and history.

There are two petroglyphs our project highlights: One of a spiral, and one of what we believe is a solar eclipse. The spiral carving is related to the summer solstice, and the eclipse carving was likely a communal record of a total solar eclipse. The spiral carving is positioned in the path of a mountain's shadow. Before and after the summer solstice, the tip of the Triangular Mountain's shadow intersects with the center of the spiral carving.



Interaction and preservation are opposites. Stone is eroded over time when it is touched and played with. The carvings in Chaco Canyon are now protected and closed off to the public for this reason. They are only accessible through images and visual mediums. This limits these ancient stellar stories to a purely visual experience, and excludes blind and low vision learners.

We wanted to create a way for everyone to be able to learn about the Puebloan culture, and to be able to experience the wonder of space and history in a new way.



Our project was to create 3D, tactile replications of these stellar carvings created by the native peoples in Chaco Canyon national park.

Through interactable, resin casted tablets, we hope to make this piece of native astronomy more accessible to the public, including blind and low vision learners. Our replicated carvings are being used in a larger project centered around the study of the sun, in collaboration with NASA's PUNCH team, SouthWest Research Institute, and FISKE planetarium.
